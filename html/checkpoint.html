About

Elon Musk, born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa, showed an early passion for technology
and entrepreneurship. After moving to the U.S., he attended the University of Pennsylvania, earning
degrees in physics and economics. Musk's career began with the co-founding of Zip2, a software
company sold for nearly $300 million. He then founded X.com, which became PayPal and was acquired
by eBay for $1.5 billion. In 2002, Musk founded SpaceX to reduce space transportation costs and
pursue Mars colonization. He also became a key figure at Tesla, revolutionizing the electric vehicle
industry. Musk's ventures expanded to include SolarCity, Neuralink, and The Boring Company,
showcasing his commitment to innovation and sustainability. Despite numerous challenges, Musk
remains a pivotal figure in modern technology and industry.

Work

  1. Zip2:Co-founded in 1996, a city guide software for newspapers.
